# Reasoning & Verbosity

Suprvisr's upgraded language model supports **multi-level reasoning** — it can think more deeply, source more documentation, and build responses you can act on with greater confidence. Alongside this, **verbosity modes** let you decide how much depth you want: a quick summary or a thorough, detailed response.

## Multi-level reasoning

When you ask a complex question, Suprvisr doesn't just jump to an answer. With multi-level reasoning enabled, the AI:

1. **Breaks down the problem** — identifies what it needs to answer fully
2. **Sources documentation** — pulls from your connected data sources and internal knowledge
3. **Builds step-by-step** — constructs the answer in layers, checking each step
4. **Surfaces its work** — you can see the reasoning process as it happens (in the Thinking panel)

This is especially useful for:

- Complex analytical questions
- Multi-step technical problems
- Answers that require reconciling multiple documents
- High-stakes decisions where you need to understand the reasoning, not just the conclusion

<Callout type="tip">
  For simple questions, reasoning adds overhead. Save deeper reasoning for questions where accuracy and thoroughness really matter.
</Callout>

## Verbosity modes

Verbosity controls **how long and detailed** the AI's response is.

| Mode | Best for | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| **Quick** | Fast answers, simple questions | Short, direct, no fluff |
| **Balanced** | Most everyday use | Clear answer with enough context |
| **Verbose** | Deep research, complex topics | Long, thorough, fully explained |

### Quick mode

Gets out of the way. No lengthy preambles, no restating the question, no caveats — just the answer. Ideal when you know what you're asking and need a fast response.

### Balanced mode

The default. Provides a direct answer with enough surrounding context to be useful and actionable.

### Verbose mode

For when you need the full picture. Long-form, well-structured responses that explain reasoning, cover edge cases, and cite sources. Use this for research, reports, or any output you'll share with others.

---

## Reasoning visibility

Regardless of verbosity mode, you can always see the AI's reasoning in the **Thinking panel** during generation. This shows:

- What the AI is working through
- What sources it's pulling from
- Observation results from tool calls

<Callout type="info">
  Reasoning visibility helps you catch errors early — if the AI is reasoning from a wrong assumption, you can stop it and redirect before it finishes.
</Callout>
