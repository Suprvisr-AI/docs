# Workspaces

Workspaces let you organize your AI interactions around specific projects. Group related chats and documents together, invite teammates to collaborate, and chat with the AI in a shared, threaded context — everyone working from the same information.

## What is a Workspace?

A Workspace is a project folder with a shared context. It contains:

- **Chats** — threaded AI conversations relevant to the project
- **Documents** — files and context assigned to the workspace
- **Team members** — collaborators who share the same AI context
- **Threaded discussions** — structured AI conversations scoped to the project

Think of it as a project room where the AI already knows the background, and your team can pick up the conversation where anyone left off.

---

## Creating a Workspace

<Steps>

### Open the Workspaces panel

Navigate to **Workspaces** in the sidebar (or use the Workspaces icon).

### Create a new workspace

Click **+ New Workspace** and give it a name — typically a project, client, or initiative.

### Add context

Assign existing chats, upload documents, or link files as context for this workspace. The AI uses this context when answering questions inside the workspace.

### Invite collaborators

Add team members by name or email. They'll see the workspace and can participate in threaded chats with the shared context.

</Steps>

---

## Threaded AI chat

Inside a Workspace, conversations are threaded — meaning the AI maintains continuity across the whole team's input, not just your own messages. Multiple teammates can ask follow-up questions and the AI builds on the full shared context.

## Assigning documents as context

Documents assigned to a workspace become part of the shared knowledge base the AI draws from. You can assign:

- Uploaded files (PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets)
- SharePoint documents (if SharePoint is connected)
- AI-generated Cards from previous chats

<Callout type="info">
  The AI prioritizes workspace context when answering questions inside that workspace. This makes answers more relevant and specific to your project.
</Callout>
